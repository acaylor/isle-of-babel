# Vision

## What this game is

Isle of Babel is a small, quiet, Myst-flavored first-person exploration game.
You arrive by rowboat in a wizard's pocket dimension — a green island adrift
in a vast lake, ringed by forested mountains — and everything you learn, you
learn by looking, walking, and reading. There is no combat, no timer, no
quest log, and nobody talks to you. The world itself is the narrator.

Three commitments define the project:

1. **Wonder over challenge.** The player should feel like a guest in a place
   that existed before they arrived and doesn't need them. Discoveries are
   rewards for curiosity — following a stream the wrong way, leaving the
   trail because something glows between the trees — never checklist items.

2. **The story lives in objects and texts.** The wizard's story is told by a
   ruin, a journal left open mid-entry, a carved boundary stone, five books
   the forest refused to give back, and a stranger's note that raises more
   questions than it answers. Even the infinite library's generated books
   are written to *hang together* — one subject threaded through title,
   chapter, and page — so browsing rewards attention.

3. **Everything is procedural, and the repository is pure text.** Every
   mesh, material, and page is generated by code at runtime. This is partly
   aesthetic (the hand-computed, low-poly-but-detailed look), partly
   practical (diffs are reviewable, the repo stays tiny), and partly the
   point: a library containing every book ever written *should* be generated
   rather than authored.

The name is a nod to Borges' *Library of Babel* — the tower's interior is an
endless library that genuinely never ends — crossed with the island logic of
Myst: a small, dense place where every object earns its position.

## The story so far (for those who'd rather read it here than find it)

> Spoilers for the current content, obviously.

The wizard built his First Tower on the forested shore and filled it with
every book he could carry. The forest — which is also a library, and was
there first — read the books slowly, the way roots read ground, and kept
them. The tower fell. Conceding the shore, the wizard folded a pocket
dimension, lined it with shelves that do not end, hung it inside a new tower
on an island he made himself ("islands hold no grudges; I will make sure of
it"), and charmed the rowboat to know the way. He left his journal on the
ruin's lectern so the forest wouldn't get the last word.

Five books remain grown into the living trees. Returning all five wakes the
ruin's cold portal ring — the wizard's last charm. And someone else is
living in the forest: their fire is cold, their note is unsigned, and they
did not come by boat. They came out of a door in the library that they
haven't been able to find again.

## Features shipped

### v0.1.0-alpha — the island and the library (2026-06-12)

- Explorable island: noise-based terrain, animated water shader, wooden
  dock, stone path, standing-stone circle with a humming crystal, and the
  wizard's tower.
- The infinite library: gallery cells stream in around the player and never
  end, each deterministically furnished from its grid coordinates.
- Browsable shelves producing generated titles, authors, and excerpts.
- The portal loop: tower door ↔ library entrance; library dais ↔ summit
  balcony.
- Detailed procedural nature: sculpted pines and broadleafs, mossy boulders,
  grass, wildflowers, unique ridged mountains with snow caps.
- First-person controller, fade transitions with spawn-point routing,
  lake-fall rescue, and the headless smoke test.

### v0.2.0-alpha — interactivity (2026-06-12)

- The open-book reading interface: leather cover, two parchment pages,
  coherent generated text; `F` leafs further.
- The paper guide bird (`Q`): the library lends you a page that folds itself
  into wings and leads you home.
- Main menu floating over the live island, camera slowly orbiting the tower.
- Pause menu; the crystal folds the world and drops you on the summit.

### v0.3.0-alpha — the voyage (2026-07-01)

- The rowboat sails: sit in it and it rows itself across the lake — free to
  look around the whole way — to the far shore, and back again with the
  island rising out of the haze.
- The far shore: an old-growth forest noticeably larger than the island,
  with a winding stream, a cairn-marked trail, and an arched footbridge.
- The ruin of the First Tower: broken ring walls, spilled columns, a cracked
  dais, a portal ring gone cold — and the wizard's story, written rather
  than generated (the boundary stone and the four-entry journal).
- Walkability overhaul (55° slopes, ground snap, ramps not lips, grounded
  bridges) and walk-tests in the smoke suite to keep it that way.

### v0.4.0-alpha — the secrets (2026-07-06)

- The five kept books, glowing in living trees off the trail, each with its
  own forest-annotated text; returning all five wakes the ruin's ring into
  a one-way door home.
- The spring glade at the stream's secret source: waterfall, mossy pool,
  drifting motes.
- The hermit's hollow: a cold camp inside a split tree, and the stranger's
  note.
- Natural boundaries: meandering rock cliffs and headlands instead of a
  walkable map edge — no invisible walls anywhere.
- A visual pass over every interactable: breathing glows, orbiting pages,
  carved stone, the open journal, the boat's lit bow lantern.

## Ideas for the future

Collected from development notes and the lore's own loose threads. Nothing
here is committed; think of it as the parts box.

### Near — the obvious next steps

- **Ambient audio.** The single biggest missing sense: lapping water, wind
  in old growth, the particular hush of infinite shelves, birdsong, the
  crystal's hum, the waterfall. All procedurally generated or synthesized,
  in keeping with the text-only repo.
- **Save/load.** Persist position, scene, and — most importantly — which
  words the forest has already given back, with a Continue button on the
  title screen. The kept-book progress being per-visit is the current
  system's one honest embarrassment.
- **Settings.** Mouse sensitivity, FOV, invert-Y. Small, overdue.

### Mid — deepening what exists

- **Library secrets.** The generated books already gossip about "a door on
  the ninth landing that opens only for students of X" and "the First
  Library" — make some of it true. Rare glowing volumes deep in the stacks;
  a coordinate-based landmark far from the entrance that rewards a long
  walk; shelving that subtly organizes by subject the further you go.
- **The stranger.** The hollow's note is a deliberately opened thread: they
  entered the forest through a library door that "the wizard never counted"
  and can't find it again. Finding that door — from either side — is the
  natural next chapter. They ask you not to tell the wizard; perhaps the
  game should notice whether you read the journal *after* the note.
- **The boat, unhurried.** An option to take the tiller yourself for the
  crossing (the enchanted autopilot stays the default), and perhaps a slow
  circumnavigation mode for looking at the island from the water.
- **Weather and light.** A slow day/night or weather drift — mist mornings
  on the lake, rain that makes the library's hush audible — as mood, not
  simulation.

### Far — expansions the lore already permits

- **Other shores.** The moss-corrected atlas says of the island map:
  *"others to follow."* The lake is large; the mountains have gaps.
- **The wizard.** Present so far only in handwriting. Whether he should ever
  appear in person is genuinely undecided — absence is doing a lot of work.
- **The forest as reader.** The kept books suggest the forest annotates what
  it keeps. A system where the forest slowly "annotates" things the player
  leaves or does would be the most ambitious expression of the theme.
- **Photo mode.** The capture harness (`tests/capture.tscn`) is already
  most of one; making it a player-facing feature is mostly UI.
- **Gamepad support** and broader accessibility.

## Non-goals

Things this project has decided *not* to be, so future contributions can
push in the right direction:

- No combat, health, hunger, or fail states beyond falling in the lake
  (which politely returns you to the dock).
- No voice acting, no NPCs with dialogue trees. If someone speaks, it will
  be in writing, probably left on a lectern.
- No binary assets in git without a very good reason (LFS is pre-routed for
  the day a good reason arrives — most likely audio).
- No open-world sprawl. Small, dense, and hand-shaped by code beats large
  and empty.
